The Local Authority District of Bath and North East Somerset has a care home investment potential score of 8.80, placing it in the "Consider" category. This score is influenced by several key factors. The district exhibits a strong positive impact in terms of House Price Growth, indicating a robust property market. Additionally, the Gross Disposable Household Income per head is relatively high, suggesting economic stability and potential affordability for care services. However, the percentage of care homes rated as "Good" by the Care Quality Commission is lower than optimal, indicating room for improvement in the quality of care provided. Furthermore, the presence of a notable percentage of care homes requiring improvement raises concerns about overall service standards. While Bath and North East Somerset shows promise with positive indicators like House Price Growth and GDHI per head, the need for enhancements in CQC ratings highlights areas for development. Therefore, this district may be considered for investment after addressing quality of care issues to enhance its overall attractiveness to investors.
